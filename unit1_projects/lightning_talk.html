Why I Watch (and thoroughly enjoy) Adventure Time

My favorite episode is "I Remember You". I remember when it first aired and I watched it, I almost cried. The only reason I didn't actually cry was because I was watching it with people and public displays of crying make me uncomfortable.

"I Remember You" tells back story of two very different characters. One is Marceline the Vampire Queen. She is a thousand years old, and sometimes loses sight of her moral code. But much of the time, she's just an angsty teenager. The other is The Ice King (once known as Simon). He is the often referred to as the biggest weirdo in the show and his madness and longevity come from a magical crown he wears. You often pity his character as he desperately searches for companionship by capturing princesses.

Prior to this episode, that's really all you know about the two characters. And then suddenly, the show reveals that the Ice King was once kind and intelligent. He saved Marceline in the aftermath of a terrible apocalyptic war when she was a little girl.

Throughout the episode, Marceline struggles to accept that The Ice King is completely nuts and no longer remembers her. He just wants to sing with her, so he pulls out his journal full of lyrics. As they sing, you realize that the journal was written by the Ice King before he became lost in his own mind. The "lyrics" are actually letters to Marceline apologizing for his decent into lunacy and the things he says.

Now, that's some heavy stuff. Keep in mind that these episodes are only 11 minutes long including the theme song. This episode's theme has strong parallels to my experience watching my Great Grandmother struggle with dementia. When she couldn't remember me at times and hid her bananas behind the curtains instead of in the fridge- I began to avoid visiting her. But as Marceline says

"You know I'm actually glad to see you. Maybe I'm the one who's...Nuts."
It's powerful and rare for a children's show to delve into friendship the serious emotional turmoil of losing that relationship. And yet, the episode is filled with humor and enough childlike innocence to keep it a PG affair.

And that is why I watch Adventure Time.
