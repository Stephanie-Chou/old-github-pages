The Stereotype Threat

I actually touched on this subject in a previous blog post when I wrote about an issue that the tech industry faces which is a lack of women. As a female engineering student at a career fair, I often heard from people (even friends) that "Girls have no problem getting a job because they are girls. Everyone hires girls." I think they meant this in an encouraging way. I think what they meant to say is "Don't worry about getting the job. You have a lot of great qualities that recruiters target."

However, specifically mentioning gender in relation to getting an interview or a job diminishes the accomplishment. If I choose to believe that employers are actively seeking women to the point where gender overrides a lot my resume, then it begins to become hard to feel as if I have earned anything.

And I think these stereotype threats are part of what is called 'The Pipeline Problem.' Over and over, you hear that there aren't enough minorties in tech. Here is a great example: At Google and Yahoo, an overwhelming majority of their workforce are male. They are only the only two large tech companies to reveal their diversity statistics.
