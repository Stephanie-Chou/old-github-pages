Conflict

  1. Discuss a time you had an emotional conflict with someone. What was it about? What happened?

    2. I had to google emotional conflict to find out what experience I have had that might qualify. An emotional conflict is

    "the presence of different and opposing emotions relating to a situation that has recently taken place or is in the process of being unfolded".

    Without getting too personal...? I don't like conflict and I tend to avoid conflict with people.

    I had a summer internship where I was leading a project. I had less experience than most of the team technically but I had more confidence to lead, so I was appointed 'team leader'. One member of the team felt he was being helpful and inqusitive. I felt he was continually off task and distant from the team. I also had a sense that he paid me unnecessary attention which made me slightly uncomfortable.

  2. What was the source of the conflict?

    3. Lack of communication or poor communication tends to be the source of most conflicts and frustrations. In this situation, I wanted to distance myself as much as possible from this guy because of the unwanted attention but if you distance yourself from a team member, that only makes the communication worse. That also worsens his contribution to the team since.

  3. How did you handle that conflict?

    4. How to mediate your emotional conflicts in the workplace.

    I tried to ignore his attentions and delegate him simple, straightforward but very needed tasks. He worked well with a to do list to accomplish. I had to be careful not to let my frustration and annoyance show when speaking to him.

  4. Did your actions make the conflict better or worse?

    5. He got his work done, so that was good. Because I knew this was a summer internship and I wouldn't have to work with him again after a few weeks, it was easier to deal with the situation in the way that I did.

  5. If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

    6. Had this been a longer term project and if I were to work with him again, (which I actually did eventually do) I would talk to him if I felt his attention was really getting in the way of my ability to work with him. I would ask him to reflect on his work so far and ask him where I or the rest of the team could help him. I would also let him know my side and tell him 'I am getting this vibe from you, it's uncomfortable whether intentional or not".

  6. What did you learn from this experience?

    7. I learned how to work with a team member who is not really acting as part of the team. I also learned that I don't respond very well if one person really frustrates me. Since I am aware of this, I have learned to control the feeling and recognize my own frustration when it happens. This helped a lot when I had to work with this person again.
