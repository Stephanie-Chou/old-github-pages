Learning Style

My thinking style was identified as concrete sequential. According to this I like:

  • following directions
  • order
  • getting facts

    • I always find that I like mentorship and someone to ask questions and get clarification. I definitely think in terms of setting parameters or deliverables. I think part of this is a product of my personality an a lot of these tendencies are a product of my education as an engineer. In engineering, there is a lot of facts and directions. In projects, there are specifications which need to be followed.

    Apparently I have difficulty with:

  • working in groups
  • lack of clarity, direction or unpredictability
  • ambiguity

    • I don't usually find that I have problems working in groups. However, I do get frustrated in group situations where the group has no organization. For example, I think it is easier to work in groups where each member has specific, actionable tasks. I also work best with semi-set timelines and goals. I know others work better with more flexibility.

    I think a lot of this is reflective of difficulty working with unpredictability and lack of direction.

    learning preferences

  • structure!

    • I do like structured environments for learning. This is part of the reason I started to look into dev bootcamps- I felt they would provide more structure for me to learn. When I tell peers that I am going to a Dev Bootcamp for Web development, a few with CS backgrounds just tell me "isn't thata something you can learn on your own." I find that a frustrating, and even somewhat arrogant comment. Apparently they haven't learned about different learning styles! I think what Dev Bootcamp provides that really helps is a community to help, goals and projects. It's hard for me to create my own projects and goals. I think this may be reflective of difficulty when asked to 'use my imagination.'
